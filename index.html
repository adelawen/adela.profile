About Me

I grow by
listening, then doing.

"Every person carries a perspective I haven't thought of yet — and that gap is where I learn the most."

Growing up, the most meaningful growth never came from sitting alone with a textbook — it came from conversations with people from entirely different backgrounds. There's something irreplaceable about genuinely listening to someone's experience, letting their input reshape how you see a problem, then articulating your own view back — sharpened by the exchange.

When I encounter something difficult — professional software no course teaches, a field I've never touched, a competition topic far outside my expertise — my instinct isn't to wait for permission. It's to find the people who know it, and start building.

When I committed to the NSTC research proposal, I had zero experience with PTV Vissim — a paid, industry-grade microsimulation platform. I learned it myself online. When I realized the road network needed to exactly match the 400m specs in the proposal, I taught myself AutoCAD to draft it precisely. Not because anyone asked — because approximate wasn't good enough.

The same drive brings me to business competitions: not for trophies, but because they force me to step inside someone else's pain and think from the inside out. Feeling where it actually hurts — and designing something that relieves that specific pressure — is the real challenge.

Core Operating Principle

I don't need to already know something to start. I need to understand why it matters — and that's enough. Whether it's a microsimulation platform, a carbon model, or a business pitch for a problem I've never lived — the process is always: immerse, question, build, iterate.
